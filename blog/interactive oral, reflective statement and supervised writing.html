Interactive Oral, Reflective Statements and Supervised Writing

The three assignments that comes along with the novel

English

 

One of the most frequent writing that IB students will do in English will be reflective statements and Supervised writing


Reflective Statements are written after Interactive Oral (presentation) since it's based on what you have learned in that presentation. So basically every time your English class reads a novel, your teacher will make you do an Interactive Oral probably in a group of two or three.  Interactive Oral is not Interactive Oral Presentation (IOP).  
Interactive Orals are generally not marked.  But you should provide enough information about the given topics for reflective statements.  It has to be interactive, so have some discussions and hands on activities.

Reflective Statements should be around 300 to 400 words total.  As this writing is about what you learned from the presentation, it is always a good idea to use first person (I).  And relate the information you learned from the presentation to the stories in the novel.

Supervised Writing should be about 1200 words.  Your teacher will provide you at least 4 topics to write about.  You should be given the full hour to write it.  So theoretically you should write one word every 3 seconds.  To help fill the word count, make sure to research useful quote in the novel and use them effectively depending on the given topics.

Estimated Schedule of IB student in English
  • Sep 10 - Start reading the Novel
  • Sep 14 - Teacher assign groups for Interactive Oral
  • Sep 18 - First half of the assigned group presents
  • Sep 23 - Write a reflective statement based on the previous presentations
  • Sep 27 - Last half of the assigned group presents
  • Oct 2 - Write a reflective statement based on the last half of the presentation
  • Oct 8 - Write a Supervised Writing
    For sample of Reflective Statement, visit http://amundsenhs.org/ourpages/auto/2013/9/23/41305762/Reflective%20Statement%20Samples.pdf
