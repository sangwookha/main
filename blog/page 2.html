Hess's Law

Chemistry HL 12

October 14, 2016 Bryan

Textbook definition of Hess's law: The enthalpy change for any chemical reaction is independent of the route provided the starting conditions and final conditions, and reactants and products, are the same. This law basically means that all the heat change in a chemical reaction is the same.  And we use this theory to calculate enthalpy of a specific chemical equation using 3 other chemical equation because it is not possible to do the experiment on that chemical equation.
